## What is the Conquest of Azeroth Travel Guide?

The Travel Guide is a specialized vanity item that grants your account access to the "Rexxar" persistent test realm (PTR). Unlike temporary open beta servers that appear during specific testing windows, the Rexxar server is a long-term environment where the developers at Project Ascension test new abilities, class balance, and world changes. 

According to player experience reports, the Rexxar server is often the first place to see "Phase" updates, such as level cap increases and the introduction of high-level talent essences. By using the guide on a live server, you flag your account for PTR access, allowing you to download the necessary client files and log into the custom expansion.

| Feature | Rexxar (Travel Guide Required) | Drexthar (Open Beta) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Access Cost** | Requires Travel Guide (DP or Gold) | Free during testing windows |
| **Server Status** | Persistent (Rarely wiped) | Temporary (Wiped after beta) |
| **Level Cap** | Usually higher/End-game focused | Often capped (e.g., Level 58) |
| **Population** | Dedicated testers/Guilds | High influx of casual players |
| **Stability** | Experimental (Daily changes) | More stable for testing stress |

## How to Acquire and Use the Guide

Acquiring the guide can be done through two primary methods: the official web store or the in-game economy. For players who prefer not to spend real money, the quest for the conquest of azeroth travel guide often leads to the Auction House on the main classless realms like Area 52.

### Acquisition Methods

1.  **The Web Store:** You can purchase the bundle directly using Donation Points (DP). This is the fastest method and ensures you get all the associated vanity rewards immediately.
2.  **The Auction House:** Players who have purchased the guide with DP can list the physical item for Gold. This allows free-to-play users to "farm" their way into the alpha/beta testing phases.
3.  **Giveaways:** Community reports suggest that Project Ascension occasionally hosts Discord giveaways or social media contests where Alpha access is granted to lucky participants.

Once you have the conquest of azeroth travel guide in your inventory, follow these steps to unlock the server:

## The "Vanity Sync" Controversy and Resolution

A common point of confusion in the community involves how items from the test realm carry over to the live release. Traditionally, Project Ascension uses a "Vanity Sync" system. When a seasonal server ends, players often have to pay a small fee or use a specific item to "sync" their earned vanity items to the permanent Area 52 server.

However, for the conquest of azeroth travel guide, the rules are slightly different. Community managers have recently clarified that the specific vanity items included in the Travel Guide bundle (like the mounts and pets) will be applied to player accounts on the live release without the need for a separate Vanity Sync purchase. 

This is a major relief for new players who were worried about a "double paywall." While you will still need a Vanity Sync if you want to bring *other* items (like store-bought transmogs from Area 52) over to the new CoA live server, the items that define the Travel Guide are yours to keep across the transition.

## FAQ: Conquest of Azeroth Travel Guide

### Does the progress on the Rexxar server carry over to the live release?
No, all character progress on the Rexxar test realm will be wiped before the official live launch of Conquest of Azeroth. Everyone will start at level 1 to ensure a fair "fresh" start for the community. However, your vanity items (mounts, pets, titles) from the bundle will be linked to your account.

### Can I buy the conquest of azeroth travel guide with in-game gold?
Yes, the Travel Guide is a tradable item. Many players purchase it from the web store and list it on the Auction House of servers like Area 52. The gold price fluctuates based on the server's economy, but it is a viable way for free-to-play players to gain access to the expansion.

### What happens to the 30-slot bag when the server resets?
The 30-slot bag included in the conquest of azeroth travel guide bundle is typically a non-vanity, Bind on Equip item. This means it does not save to your account-wide collection. Once the test realm is wiped or your character is deleted, the bag will likely be lost unless the developers decide to change its item classification in the future.

### Do I need a Vanity Sync to use my Travel Guide mounts on the live server?
According to recent community manager confirmations, the vanity items specifically included in the conquest of azeroth travel guide will be available on the live server without requiring an additional Vanity Sync. However, if you want to bring over other vanity items you've collected on other Ascension realms, you will likely need to purchase a standard Vanity Sync.

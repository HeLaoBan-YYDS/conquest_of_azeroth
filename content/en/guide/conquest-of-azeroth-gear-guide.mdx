## Understanding Early Progression and Loot

The journey from level 1 to 60 on this custom realm is vastly different from traditional experiences. You aren't just relying on random mob drops; the world itself is filled with opportunities to customize your character’s power.

### World Forged Items and RPG Loot
Players often overlook the environment, but the world is littered with "World Forged" items. These are manually placed objects—ranging from bags to powerful weapons—hidden in caves and corners of Azeroth. Additionally, the game features "RPG Loot," where humanoid enemies drop the specific armor or weapons they are visibly wearing.

| Loot Type | Source | Best Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| World Forged | Hidden in the world | Stat boosts & unique buffs |
| RPG Loot | Humanoid Mobs | Transmog & specific weapon farming |
| Quest Rewards | Standard Questing | Baseline progression |
| Profession Gear | Crafted by players | High-stat leveling upgrades |

According to community reports, if you are looking for a specific weapon type like a two-handed sword or staff, farming humanoids that wield those items is one of the most efficient ways to gear up during the early levels.

## The Power of Professions

Unlike standard World of Warcraft, professions here are not just a secondary hobby; they are a core component of your gear progression. Crafted items are significantly more powerful than standard gear found at similar levels because they roll with additional random affixes.

Even better, these items can be upgraded twice. The first upgrade provides a stat boost, while the second upgrade increases the item's rarity to Rare (blue), making them essential for maintaining a competitive edge. Because the world is scalable, you can gather materials from lower-level zones while still earning experience, turning crafting into a seamless, rewarding loop.

## The Endgame Gearing Loop

Once you reach the level cap, the real challenge begins. The endgame is structured to provide multiple paths to greatness, whether you enjoy raiding, dungeons, or high-stakes PvP.

### Call Boards and Catch-Up Mechanics
Capital city call boards are your primary source for daily tasks. Completing these quests rewards you with Runes of Ascension and fills a progress bar that grants a "cache of loot." This cache is a fantastic catch-up mechanic, as it often provides gear from the previous tier, allowing new players or alts to gear up quickly.

| Activity | Primary Reward | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Call Board Dailies | Loot Caches / Runes | Easy |
| Heroic Dungeons | Dungeon Gear | Moderate |
| Mythic Plus | Best-in-Slot Potential | High |
| Raids | Tier Set Tokens | Very High |

As noted in [player experience](https://ascension.gg/), you do not need to tag a mob to receive credit for these quests—simply touching it once is enough. This makes the open-world questing experience highly cooperative and efficient.

## Mastering Mythic Plus and Raiding

For those seeking the absolute best gear on the realm, Mythic Plus and raiding are the pinnacles of progression. Mythic Plus requires you to travel to the dungeon entrance and use a key on a pedestal. These runs provide currency that can be used to upgrade your existing gear, adding a layer of strategic depth to your progression.

### Raid Progression Strategy
Raiding follows a traditional tier structure. You will start by collecting tokens from bosses, which can be exchanged in the main city for powerful tier sets. 

*   **Pro Tip:** Killing just one boss in a raid tier often unlocks the entire set for purchase at the vendor, significantly speeding up your gearing process.
*   **Strategic Upgrades:** Always prioritize upgrading your Mythic Plus items, as they have the potential to become the best-in-slot (BiS) gear available.

## PvP Gearing and High-Risk Zones

PvP on this realm is not just for show; it is an integrated part of the gearing journey. While structured battlegrounds and arenas are the standard, the "High-Risk" PvP zones offer a unique twist.

In these zones, dying to a player carries the risk of losing some of your items. To balance this, mobs in these areas have a higher chance of dropping dungeon-quality loot. This allows you to recover quickly if you fall in battle. Furthermore, endgame high-risk zones provide rare crafting materials essential for creating top-tier gear, making them a lucrative, albeit dangerous, destination for geared players.

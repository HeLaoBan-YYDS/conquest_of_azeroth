## Getting Started: Character Creation and Custom Classes

The first thing any player will notice is the sheer volume of choices on the character creation screen. Unlike standard World of Warcraft, Conquest of Azeroth introduces 21 entirely custom classes, each featuring three to four distinct specializations. This includes the introduction of support roles that offer a fresh dynamic to group play. Community reports suggest that reading the in-game descriptions thoroughly is the best way to avoid "restart fatigue" later on.

Before you enter the world, you must make a few critical choices regarding your gameplay experience. You can toggle the "Scalable World" feature, which ensures that mobs remain relevant to your level. Unlike retail scaling, you can still outlevel enemies by up to four levels, maintaining a sense of power progression while preventing zones from becoming obsolete. Additionally, you can choose whether or not to view other players' transmogrifications, a feature that caters to those who prefer a more grounded visual experience.

## The Path to Level 10: Early Progression Mechanics

Your primary goal in the early game is reaching level 10. This milestone unlocks the Character Advancement window (accessed via the 'N' key), where the true depth of the realm begins to show. In the starting zones, look for the "Books of Ascension" near quest givers. These NPCs provide the Path of Ascension quests, which serve as a tutorial for the realm's unique systems.

Completing these tasks rewards you with Runes of Ascension. This currency is vital for your journey, as it is used to reset talents and upgrade specialized gear. While some veteran players find these tutorial quests repetitive, the currency gain is too significant to ignore. Player experience indicates that hoarding these runes early on allows for much more experimentation with your build once you reach the middle levels.

One of the best quality-of-life improvements in this realm is the availability of mounts at level 5. While it is only a 40% speed increase, it drastically reduces travel time between early quest hubs. You can find these mounts at the stables in the second village of your starting zone.

## Exploration and World Forged Items

Exploration is heavily rewarded in this version of Azeroth. The developers have manually placed "World Forged" items in hidden corners, caves, and previously empty locations. These items range from simple bags to powerful unique weapons and armor. If you see an object sparkling on the ground, it is almost always worth the detour.

Furthermore, the realm utilizes an "RPG Loot" system. This means that humanoid mobs have a chance to drop the exact equipment they are wearing or wielding. While this is a fantastic way to collect transmog appearances, it is also a viable way to find weapon upgrades while leveling. If you need a specific weapon type, such as a two-handed sword, hunting NPCs that use that weapon is a legitimate strategy.

A comprehensive **conquest of azeroth leveling guide** must emphasize that the fastest way to gain experience is through open-world questing and exploration. While other methods exist, the sheer density of new locations and World Forged treasures makes questing the most lucrative path for both gold and gear.

## Talent Trees and Specialization Perks

At level 10, you gain access to your talent trees. The layout is inspired by modern expansion designs, featuring a dual-tree system. The left side focuses on class-wide utility and core abilities, while the right side is dedicated to your chosen specialization. You receive one point for each side every time you level up.

As you invest points into your specialization tree, you will unlock unique perks at specific level thresholds. These perks often define the playstyle of your spec, providing passive bonuses or powerful new active abilities.

Because this is a custom realm with frequent balance passes, there are rarely "cookie-cutter" builds that stay dominant for long. Players are encouraged to use their Runes of Ascension to experiment with different talent combinations.

## Leveling Activities: Mana Storms and Dungeons

Once you pass the initial levels, you have several options for how to proceed. Mana Storms are a popular choice for those who prefer solo play. These are scalable solo dungeons that do not require a standard tank/healer/DPS composition. Inside, you are provided with special potions for healing, crowd control removal, and resurrection.

However, recent community reports suggest that the experience gains from Mana Storms have been significantly reduced to encourage world exploration. While they remain excellent for obtaining unique cosmetics and specific currencies, they are no longer the "speed leveling" kings they once were.

The Random Dungeon Finder (RDF) becomes available at level 15. This follows the standard Wrath of the Lich King format, teleporting you directly to the instance. While RDF provides good gear and a break from questing, the experience rewards have also been adjusted to ensure the open world remains populated.

## The Role of Professions in Leveling

Professions are far more integrated into the leveling process here than in standard WoW. Both gathering and crafting provide experience points, making them a viable supplementary leveling path. More importantly, crafted items are often superior to quest rewards of the same level.

When you craft an item, it has a chance to receive random affixes, such as extra Strength or Intellect. Furthermore, every crafted item can be upgraded twice. The second upgrade typically transforms the item into a "Rare" (blue) quality piece, significantly boosting its stats. This makes professions like Blacksmithing, Leatherworking, and Tailoring essential for players who want to maintain a high power level while moving through difficult zones.

Using a **conquest of azeroth leveling guide** to plan your professions early can save you a significant amount of gold. Many players choose to gather resources in lower-level zones while the world scaling is active, allowing them to gain XP and materials simultaneously.

## PvP Modes and High-Risk Gameplay

Ascension is known for its flexible PvP options, and Conquest of Azeroth is no exception. By accessing the Honor tab (H key), you can choose between three distinct modes. This choice dictates how you interact with the opposing faction and the risks associated with death.

1.  **Safe Haven:** You are completely protected from open-world PvP. This is ideal for players who want to focus entirely on the story and PVE progression.
2.  **No Risk:** You are flagged for PvP, allowing for skirmishes, but you lose nothing upon death.
3.  **High Risk:** This is the most intense mode. Dying to another player causes you to drop loot into a chest that the victor can pillage. To compensate for this risk, you gain access to a separate loot table from regular mobs, including dungeon-quality items.

Community reports indicate that "honorable combat" buffs have been implemented to prevent high-level players from ganking those more than five levels below them. However, if you choose High Risk, you should always be prepared for a fight.

## Reaching the Endgame: Level 60 and Prestige

Once you hit level 60, the game shifts toward traditional endgame activities like Heroic dungeons, Raiding, and Mythic Plus. However, Conquest of Azeroth introduces the "Prestige" system. If you enjoyed the leveling process or want to earn extra rewards, you can reset your character to level one.

Prestiging grants you a wealth of Runes of Ascension and special chests that can be opened once you return to level 60. These chests contain high-level gear, providing a significant head start on the endgame grind. It is a brilliant way to keep the open world alive while rewarding players for their time.

For those staying at 60, the "Call Boards" in capital cities are your primary source of daily progression. These boards offer quests that reward raid-level loot caches. Whether you are slaying specific monsters or fulfilling profession orders, the Call Boards provide a steady stream of gear and currency.

## FAQ

### What is the fastest way to level in Conquest of Azeroth?
According to most community reports, the fastest way to level is through open-world questing combined with professions. While Mana Storms and Dungeons provide great loot, their experience rewards have been tuned down to prioritize world exploration. Following a **conquest of azeroth leveling guide** that focuses on efficient quest hubs is highly recommended.

### Can I change my class after starting?
In Conquest of Azeroth, your class is fixed at character creation. However, you can freely change your specializations and talents using Runes of Ascension. If you want to try an entirely different class, such as moving from a Reaper to a Necromancer, you will need to create a new character or use a Prestige reset.

### Is High-Risk PvP required for the best gear?
No, High-Risk PvP is optional. While it offers a unique way to obtain dungeon-level loot from world mobs, you can acquire the same (and better) gear through Raiding, Mythic Plus, and the Call Board system in capital cities. High Risk is primarily for players who enjoy the adrenaline of stakes-based combat.

### How do I get more Runes of Ascension?
Runes of Ascension are earned by completing Path of Ascension quests, daily Call Board tasks, and through the Prestige process. They are a vital currency for resetting talents and upgrading your World Forged or crafted equipment, so it is wise to collect them whenever possible.

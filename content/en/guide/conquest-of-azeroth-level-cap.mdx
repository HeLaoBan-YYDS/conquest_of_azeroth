## Leveling Milestones & Custom Classes

The journey to the **conquest of azeroth level cap** is unlike any other leveling experience in World of Warcraft. Instead of the standard classless system seen on other Ascension realms, Conquest of Azeroth features 21 fully custom classes built from the ground up using Warcraft lore. These are not mere reskins; each class features completely custom spells, talent trees, specializations, icons, and visual effects.

As you level up, you will unlock power spikes and customization options at key milestones. At level 10, you unlock your class's specializations. Each class has at least three distinct specs—and some even have a fourth—allowing you to customize your character to fit your preferred playstyle. 

Conquest of Azeroth also introduces a brand-new "Support" role. This role focuses on empowering group members with buffs, mitigating incoming damage, and manipulating enemies, all while dealing damage. This creates an entirely new dynamic in both PvE and PvP group compositions.

## Leveling Challenges: Hardcore Trials and Prestige

If you are looking for a more intense journey to the **conquest of azeroth level cap**, you can opt into Hardcore Trials. These are optional, self-imposed challenge modes that completely change how you level up. You can activate these trials by speaking to the challenger NPC in your race's starting zone.

Some of the most popular community-reported trials include:
*   **Pair in the Lair:** A cooperative trial where you level up as a two-man Tinker duo. One player constructs mechs and turrets while the other focuses on healing.
*   **Lonely Artisan:** A crafting-only trial where you only gain experience by crafting items. For example, a Knight of Xoroth can drop a custom anvil to forge weapons in the wastes to level up.
*   **Gatlazoth Challenge:** A combat-heavy trial where you must defeat heavily armored foes. This is perfect for a Brigand Ranger utilizing venom blades and deepwood poisons.

Reaching the max level under these hardcore conditions rewards players with exclusive cosmetics, such as the Emberforged Defender Plate.

If you enjoy the leveling process, Conquest of Azeroth features a Prestige system. Once you hit the **conquest of azeroth level cap**, you can choose to prestige. This resets your character back to level 1 while keeping your gear, items, gold, professions, and reputations intact. 

Prestiging allows you to take on new hardcore trials or simply enjoy the leveling journey again. Upon reaching the cap again, you will receive a chest of spoils containing high-end gear and Runes of Ascension, making it a viable alternative for gearing up.

## Gearing Up: Immersive Drops, Worldforged Items, and Woodworking

Once you finally hit the **conquest of azeroth level cap**, your gearing journey truly begins. Conquest of Azeroth completely overhauls classic itemization to support its 21 custom classes and 70 specializations. 

First, the server features an RPG Creature Overhaul. All creatures in Azeroth are redesigned from the ground up based on their visual appearance. For example, plate-wearing enemies have high armor and hit hard but slow, while lightly armored bandits have high agility, crit chance, and fast attacks that disrupt spellcasting.

To complement this, the server utilizes **Immersive Gear Drops**. Creatures have a chance to drop the actual items they are wearing when slain, adding over 9,000 new items to the vanilla database. You can target-farm specific enemies to find gear suited to your class, such as hunting Stranglethorn trolls for Witch Doctor gear or Venture Co. goblins for Tinker equipment.

Additionally, players can discover over 1,800 **Worldforged Items** hidden across the world. These are clickable objects that contain unique, powerful trinkets, rings, or weapons. For example:
*   A ring for a Harvest Reaper that causes enemies to flee in panic when they strike you.
*   An amulet for a Rot Weaver Venomancer that adds petrification magic to your damage-over-time spells.
*   A blazing ring that wreaths your Hellfire Knight of Xoroth in extra flames.

At max level, you can upgrade your favorite Worldforged items using Runes of Ascension, which are earned from quests, dungeons, and PvP.

If you prefer crafting your own gear, the server features a brand-new primary/secondary profession: **Woodworking**. You can gather resources using Woodcutting and craft staves, wands, bows, crossbows, throwing weapons, shields, off-hands, and arrows. Crafted gear on Conquest of Azeroth can roll with random affixes, allowing you to forge highly specific stat combinations for your unique build.

## Endgame Content at Max Level

Reaching the **conquest of azeroth level cap** is just the beginning of your journey. Once you hit level 60, you can team up with other players to tackle the deadliest challenges in Azeroth.

### Endgame Dungeons and Mythic+
Dive into classic dungeons and brand-new custom instances, such as the Karazhan Crypts or the Road to the Other Side. All dungeons feature updated mechanics and are available on Normal, Heroic, Mythic, and Mythic+ difficulties. Mythic+ introduces scaling health, damage, and savage affixes that will test your group's coordination.

### Overhauled Raids
Raids on Conquest of Azeroth are designed for 10 to 25 players and feature completely overhauled boss mechanics. You can delve into classic raids like Molten Core, Onyxia, and Blackwing Lair to earn epic gear. For the ultimate challenge, guilds can attempt Ascended Raids, which represent the absolute pinnacle of PvE difficulty.

### World Bosses
World bosses offer a unique high-stakes challenge. You can fight them in the open world, where they become massive PvPvE brawls between competing raid groups. Alternatively, guilds can use dedicated altars to enter an instanced version of the boss, facing a stronger version in a controlled environment without outside interference.

### Heroes Callboard
For solo players, the Heroes Callboard provides endgame quests in high-level zones like Winterspring and the Plaguelands. Completing these quests rewards you with a Callboard Cache, which contains raid-equivalent gear, ensuring solo players have a reliable path to high-end progression.

### Battlegrounds and Arenas
Take your custom build into competitive PvP. Queue up for classic battlegrounds like Warsong Gulch and Arathi Basin, or compete in 2v2, 3v3, and Solo Queue arenas. The introduction of 21 custom classes and 70 specializations creates an incredibly diverse and unpredictable PvP meta.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the current Conquest of Azeroth level cap?
The final vanilla-era **conquest of azeroth level cap** is level 60. However, during alpha and beta testing phases, developers often utilize lower incremental caps (such as level 30, 35, or 50) to thoroughly test class balance and progression pacing before releasing the full endgame.

### Can I reset my level after reaching the max level?
Yes. Conquest of Azeroth features a Prestige system. Once you reach the max level, you can choose to prestige, which resets your character to level 1. You will keep your gear, gold, bank, professions, and reputations. Reaching the level cap again rewards you with a chest of spoils containing Runes of Ascension and powerful gear.

### How do I gear up after reaching the conquest of azeroth level cap?
Gearing up after hitting the **conquest of azeroth level cap** involves several progression paths. You can farm Immersive Gear Drops from specific creatures, hunt for Worldforged Items in the open world, craft specialized gear using the new Woodworking profession, complete daily Heroes Callboard quests for raid-equivalent caches, or run endgame dungeons and raids.

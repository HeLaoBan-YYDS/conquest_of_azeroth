## Getting Started: The Scalable World and Your Custom Class

Before diving headfirst into dungeons, you must understand how Conquest of Azeroth (CoA) fundamentally changes character progression. Unlike traditional World of Warcraft, CoA does not feature standard classes like Mages or Warriors. Instead, you choose from 21 entirely custom, lore-rich classes such as the Reaper, Witch Hunter, Felsworn, or Runemaster. Each class utilizes its own unique resource mechanics and boasts Dragonflight-style dual talent trees, which unlock at level 10. On the left side, you will find your class-specific talents, while the right side houses your specialization talents. To get the most out of your leveling experience, keeping this conquest of azeroth dungeon guide handy will ensure you don't miss any valuable loot.

During character creation, you can toggle the "Scalable World" feature. It is highly recommended to keep this enabled. The scaling system ensures that monsters remain challenging and rewarding, capping your power at a maximum of four levels above the zone's standard bracket. This allows you to finish questlines in your favorite zones without out-leveling them or rendering their rewards obsolete.

As you explore, keep an eye out for "World Forged" items—hand-placed chests, bags, and weapons scattered across Azeroth. Additionally, the realm features immersive RPG loot, meaning humanoid enemies have a chance to drop the exact gear and weapons they are visually wearing.

## Mana Storms: Solo Dungeon Challenges and Rewards

Mana Storms are a unique, solo-friendly dungeon experience unlocked at level 10. They act as procedural, scalable dungeons that do not require a traditional trinity group of a tank, healer, and DPS. While soloing, you might wonder how to optimize your builds; that is where a specialized conquest of azeroth dungeon guide becomes invaluable. 

Inside a Mana Storm, players are given three unique potions to compensate for the lack of a traditional group structure. These items help you survive tough encounters, heal up between fights, and recover from mistakes.

| Potion Type | Function | Cooldown / Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Healing Potion** | Restores a large portion of your health. | Short cooldown. |
| **CC Removal Potion** | Removes crowd control and movement-impairing effects. | Strategic usage during boss fights. |
| **Resurrection Potion** | Resurrects a fallen ally (if playing in a group). | Essential for multi-player runs. |

Community reports indicate that while Mana Storms were previously the absolute fastest way to level, the developers have significantly reduced their experience gains. This adjustment was made to encourage players to explore the open world, complete revamped quests, and engage with the upgraded professions system. However, Mana Storms remain an excellent source of custom cosmetic items, power-ups, and Runes of Ascension—the primary currency used to reset talents and upgrade World Forged gear.

## The Random Dungeon Finder (RDF) and Group Play

Once you hit level 15, the Random Dungeon Finder (RDF) becomes available via the default user interface. The system operates similarly to Wrath of the Lich King's dungeon finder, automatically matching you with a tank, healer, and three DPS players before teleporting you directly into the instance. When queueing up, having a reliable conquest of azeroth dungeon guide will help you understand the custom mechanics of revamped vanilla instances.

While dungeon questing is highly engaging, player experience suggests that raw XP from RDF has also been toned down. To level efficiently, you should weave dungeons between open-world questing and crafting. Professions on Conquest of Azeroth are incredibly potent. Gathering and crafting both grant character experience, and crafted items are far stronger than standard gear of the same level. Crafted gear can receive random stat affixes and can be upgraded up to two times, eventually turning into powerful rare (blue) items.

| PvP Risk Mode | Flagged Status | Death Penalty | Extra Benefits |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Safe Haven** | Unflagged (Blue) | None | Cross-faction PvE safety. |
| **No Risk** | Flagged | None | Standard open-world experience. |

If you enjoy PvP, you can select your risk level at any capital city. High Risk PvP allows you to loot gear from defeated players, but exposes you to the same threat. To protect lower-level players in the scalable world, a "Honorable Combat" buff prevents players from attacking you if they are more than five levels higher than you.

## Mythic+ Keystones and Endgame Dungeon Scaling

Upon reaching level 60, the true PvE endgame begins. All classic vanilla dungeons—including Scarlet Monastery, Deadmines, and Dire Maul—scale up to level 60. They are available in Heroic, Mythic, and Mythic+ difficulties. For those pushing high-level keystones, a detailed conquest of azeroth dungeon guide is essential for planning trash pulls and managing affixes.

Mythic+ operates on a keystone system similar to modern retail WoW. Keystones can be acquired from endgame world activities, weekly vaults, or Mana Storm caches. Completing a dungeon within the designated timer upgrades your key, while failing it degrades the level but still awards reduced loot.

| Key Level | Affix Count | Scaling Modifier | Focus Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **+2 to +3** | 0 Affixes | Base health & damage increase. | Learn basic boss mechanics and layout. |
| **+4 to +6** | 1 Affix | Moderate health & damage scaling. | Coordinate interrupts and crowd control. |
| **+7 to +9** | 2 Affixes | High health & damage scaling. | Optimize DPS rotations and healer mana. |
| **+10+** | 3+ Affixes | Extreme scaling. | Strict route planning and defensive cooldowns. |

Affixes rotate on a weekly schedule, introducing mechanics like explosive damage pulses, enraged trash mobs, and mandatory interrupt sequences. Because vanilla dungeons feature narrow corridors, positioning and crowd control are significantly more critical than in retail environments. High-level keys reward players with powerful Ability Essences and upgrading materials, allowing you to optimize your custom class builds to their absolute limits.

## Endgame Gearing: Call Boards, Professions, and Raids

Gearing up at level 60 involves multiple interconnected systems. If you want to avoid the initial gear grind, your first stop should be the capital city Call Boards. These boards offer daily PvE and profession quests. Completing these dailies fills a progression bar that ultimately rewards you with a cache of high-tier raid gear from previous tiers, serving as an exceptional catch-up mechanic. By combining daily quests with the strategies in this conquest of azeroth dungeon guide, you can fast-track your character's progression.

| Gearing Activity | Primary Rewards | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Call Boards** | Previous-tier raid caches, Runes of Ascension. | Fresh level 60 catch-up. |
| **Mythic+ Dungeons** | Scaled gear, upgrade tokens, Ability Essences. | Consistent gear progression. |
| **Raids (MC, ZG, BWL)** | Tier set tokens, legendary materials. | BiS endgame optimization. |
| **High-Risk PvP Zones** | Rare endgame crafting materials. | Wealth generation and crafting. |

For organized group PvE, raiding on Conquest of Azeroth revitalizes classic encounters. Progression begins with Zul'Gurub and Molten Core, moving into Blackwing Lair. Bosses drop tokens that can be traded at capital city vendors for custom tier sets tailored to the realm's 21 custom classes. While raids are distinct from 5-man content, our conquest of azeroth dungeon guide also bridges the gap between pre-raid BiS gearing and entry-level raiding. 

If you ever grow tired of your max-level character but love the journey, you can utilize the Prestige system. This resets your character back to level 1 in exchange for valuable endgame chests, Runes of Ascension, and cosmetic rewards once you reach level 60 again.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Where can I find the best conquest of azeroth dungeon guide for high-level keystones?
A: The most comprehensive strategies are compiled by the community on the official Project Ascension Discord and community wikis. These resources detail specific class synergies, optimal trash-skipping routes, and weekly affix workarounds.

### Q: Can I run Mana Storms with my friends, or are they strictly solo?
A: While designed with solo players in mind, Mana Storms scale dynamically. You can enter them in groups of any size without needing a dedicated tank or healer, making them a highly flexible option for casual group play.

### Q: How do I acquire my first Mythic+ keystone?
A: You can obtain your very first +2 keystone by completing the level 60 introductory PvE quest chain. Afterward, keystones can be found in weekly vault rewards, endgame world activities, and Mana Storm completion caches.

### Q: What happens if I fail to beat the timer in a Mythic+ dungeon?
A: If you fail to beat the timer, your keystone will degrade by one level. However, you will still receive loot at the end of the run, albeit at a slightly reduced efficiency compared to a successfully timed run.

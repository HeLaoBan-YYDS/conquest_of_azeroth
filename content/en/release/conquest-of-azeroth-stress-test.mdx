## What Was the Conquest of Azeroth Stress Test?

The primary objective of the latest testing phase was to push the Project Ascension server infrastructure to its absolute limits. Unlike previous Closed Alpha or Public Test Realm (PTR) phases, this event took place directly on the live launcher under the live realms section. This allowed any player with the launcher installed to log in and participate in the chaos.

To maximize server load and simulate worst-case launch day scenarios, developers implemented a strict "human-only" restriction for character creation. Player experiences shared across community channels revealed initial confusion, as many attempted to customize Blood Elves, Undead, or Eredar (Draenei) only to be met with a locked character creation screen. By forcing every single participant to spawn as a human in Northshire Abbey, the developers concentrated thousands of players into a single sub-zone.

During this specific **conquest of azeroth stress test**, developers limited character creation to ensure that the hardware was subjected to the highest possible density of player interactions, spell casting, and movement updates.

## Technical Performance and Server Hurdles

A stress test is designed to break things, and this event certainly succeeded in that regard. With hundreds of players packed into the human starting area, performance was a major talking point. Many players reported their frame rates dropping to single digits, hovering between 8 to 12 FPS even on high-end gaming rigs.

To push the hardware even further, the development team initiated several server-wide events. They spawned massive waves of level 6 Murlocs inside Northshire Abbey, sending players running for their lives. Additionally, developers repeatedly teleported every player on the realm to the exact same coordinates, resulting in instant server crashes and subsequent rapid restarts.

| Technical Issue | Community Observation | Recommended Workaround |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Low Frame Rates (8-12 FPS)** | Severe stuttering in high-density areas like Northshire Abbey. | Lower graphics settings to "Super Low" and disable player names. |
| **Bugged Spells & Missing UI** | Abilities like *Soul Rend* or *Righteous Lunge* not appearing on action bars. | Relog, disable level scaling/transmog, or try a different class temporarily. |
| **Frequent Disconnects** | Server restarts triggered by developers to clear memory and test load. | Monitor the official Discord channel for real-time status updates. |
| **Launcher Download Bottlenecks** | Slow download speeds when updating the client before logging in. | Close background applications and restart the launcher as Administrator. |

While the **conquest of azeroth stress test** was visually spectacular due to the sheer volume of players, it highlighted the necessity of optimization before the official launch. Players are hopeful that the live launch will feature smoother layer transitions to distribute the population across multiple instances of the starting zones.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will my character progress from the conquest of azeroth stress test carry over to the live server?
No, all character progress made during the stress test has been wiped. This is standard practice for testing realms to ensure a fair and equal playing field for everyone on the official launch day.

### Is Conquest of Azeroth free to play?
Yes, the expansion is completely free to play. You do not need a retail subscription or any premium purchases to download the launcher, create a character, and play on the live realms.

### Why was character creation limited to humans during the conquest of azeroth stress test?
The developers restricted character creation to the human race to force all players into Elwynn Forest. This concentrated the server load in a single zone, allowing the team to test how the hardware handles extreme player density and spell effects.

### How do I fix the low FPS issues I experienced during the test?
To improve your performance on launch day, lower your graphics settings to the minimum, disable player names in your interface settings, and make sure your graphics drivers are fully updated. Using the 64-bit client provided in the launcher can also help stabilize your frame rate.

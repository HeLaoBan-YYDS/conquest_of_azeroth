## The RPG Creature Overhaul

The world itself has undergone a massive transformation to match the power of these new classes. The RPG creature overhaul is a fundamental system that changes how every mob in Azeroth behaves. Stats are no longer arbitrary; they are based on the creature's physical appearance.

If you encounter a bandit wearing heavy plate armor and wielding a two-handed mace, that enemy will have high physical defense and slow, crushing attacks. Conversely, a rogue-like enemy in leather gear will have high dodge rates and fast attacks that can interrupt your spell casting. This forces players to adapt their strategy based on the specific enemy they are facing.

| Creature Attribute | Effect on Combat | Strategic Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Plate Armor** | High physical mitigation, lower magic resist | Use magic damage or armor penetration. |
| **Leather/Cloth** | High agility, faster attack speed, high crit | Use stuns, shields, or kiting mechanics. |
| **Two-Handed Weapons** | High burst damage, slower swing timer | Timing blocks or parries is essential. |
| **Dual Wielding** | High hit frequency, interrupts casters | Use instant-cast spells or high-armor tanks. |

## Immersive Loot and Worldforged Items

Gearing up in the conquest of azeroth full release is more interactive than ever before. The "Immersive Item Drops" system means that enemies have a chance to drop the actual gear they are wearing. If you see a troll witch doctor wielding a cool-looking staff, there is a legitimate chance you can loot that exact item. This adds over 9,000 new items to the vanilla database.

Furthermore, the world is scattered with over 1,800 Worldforged items. These are clickable objects hidden in thematic locations—campsites, ruins, or secret caverns—that contain unique equipment with powerful effects.

*   **Sinister Petrification:** An amulet found on a Rotweaver that turns enemies to stone.
*   **Shadowflame Heart:** Boots for a Knight of Zoroth that increase movement speed while in combat.
*   **Fear-Inducing Rings:** Items that cause enemies to panic when they strike the wearer.

These items can be upgraded at max level using Runes of Ascension, allowing you to keep your favorite thematic gear throughout the entire endgame. You can find more details on these systems on the [official Project Ascension website](https://ascension.gg).

## PvP Modes and High-Risk Gameplay

The conquest of azeroth full release offers three distinct ways to experience the world, which can be toggled in any major city. This ensures that the player base remains unified on a single realm while allowing for varied playstyles.

1.  **PvE Mode:** The standard experience where you cannot be attacked by other players. You can still interact with the community and join groups for dungeons.
2.  **War Mode:** Enables open-world PvP with members of the opposite faction. This mode provides an experience bonus to reward the increased risk.
3.  **High Risk Mode:** The ultimate challenge. Players earn the highest experience bonuses and can find "Bloodforged" gear, but dying means dropping a chest containing your loot and gold.

For those who want to play a "villain" role, Mercenary Mode allows you to attack members of your own faction. This is perfect for the lore of the Cultist or the Witch Hunter, who may have motives that transcend simple faction loyalty.

## Hardcore Trials and the Prestige System

For players who find the standard leveling process too easy, the Hardcore Trials offer a way to prove their skill. These are optional challenges that can be activated at level one. Examples include the "Tinker Duo," where two players must level together using only their mechs and turrets, or the "Lonely Artisan," where experience is only gained through crafting.

If you complete a trial, you earn exclusive rewards and titles. The Prestige system then allows you to reset to level one while keeping your gear and professions, letting you tackle a different trial or simply enjoy the journey again for additional rewards and Runes of Ascension.

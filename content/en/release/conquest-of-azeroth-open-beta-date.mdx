## Leveling Mechanics and Open World Overhauls

Starting your journey on a fresh server can be daunting, but CoA introduces several quality-of-life adjustments to make leveling engaging. As early as the character creation screen, players must decide whether to enable a scalable world. Unlike retail WoW, where mobs scale directly to your level, Project Ascension’s scalable world allows you to overlevel content by up to four levels. This preserves the feeling of character progression while ensuring your favorite zones remain relevant.

To help players navigate the early game, the server offers a free 40% speed mount at level 5. This is a massive boost to early mobility, and players can upgrade to standard mounts at levels 20 and 40. Additionally, the open world is littered with "World Forged" items—sparkly, manually placed treasures containing unique gear, bags, and buffs.

Another major feature is the RPG loot system. Humanoid monsters in the open world will physically drop the weapons and armor they are wearing. If you see a bandit wielding a cool two-handed sword, killing them gives you a direct chance to loot that exact weapon for stats or transmogrification.

| Level Milestone | Unlocked Feature / Reward | Cost / Requirements |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | Hardcore & Challenge Modes | Talk to the starting zone NPC to enable Nightmare or Adventure modes. |
| **Level 5** | Basic Mount (40% Speed) | Free at the local stables; completes a Path of Ascension quest. |
| **Level 10** | Talent Trees & Mana Storms | Unlocks the dual-tree talent system and solo dungeon finder. |
| **Level 15** | Random Dungeon Finder (RDF) | Queue up for standard dungeons with players across the realm. |
| **Level 20** | Standard Mount (60% Speed) | Costs approximately 5 gold at the riding trainer. |
| **Level 40** | Epic Mount (100% Speed) | Standard gold cost; drastically increases world travel speed. |
| **Level 60** | Endgame Progression | Unlocks Raids, Mythic+, Call Boards, and the Prestige system. |

Every step of the leveling process was refined during successive test phases. The feedback gathered during each historical **conquest of azeroth open beta date** allowed developers to fine-tune the experience curve, ensuring that leveling through professions and questing feels rewarding.

## Game Modes: Mana Storms and PvP Rulesets

At level 10, players unlock Mana Storms—a unique, solo-friendly dungeon mode that scales based on your group size. Mana Storms do not require standard tanks or healers, as players receive custom potions to heal, remove crowd control, and resurrect allies. While player experience and community reports note that XP gains from Mana Storms were heavily nerfed to encourage open-world exploration, they remain an excellent source of custom cosmetic currencies and power-ups.

For PvP enthusiasts, Conquest of Azeroth offers three distinct rulesets. Players can toggle these modes at any time in capital cities via the Honor tab.

| PvP Mode | Loot Risk | Leveling Scaling | Extra Rewards & Perks |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Safe Haven** | No Risk | Enabled (Scalable) | Completely safe from world PvP; ideal for questing. |
| **No Risk** | No Risk | Enabled (Scalable) | Normal world PvP flagging with zero item loss on death. |
| **High Risk** | High Risk (Drop loot) | Enabled (Scalable) | Chance to drop gear on death; drops dungeon loot from normal mobs. |

The High Risk mode has been heavily adjusted since the early **conquest of azeroth open beta date** tests. To combat unfair ganking in the scalable world, developers implemented an "Honorable Combat" buff. High-level players cannot attack you unless they are within five levels of your character, and outside players cannot interrupt active duels.

## Reaching Level 60: The Ultimate Endgame Loop

Once you reach the level 60 cap, the true endgame begins. If you love your class but enjoy the journey of leveling, you can opt to "Prestige." Prestiging resets your character to level 1, rewarding you with Runes of Ascension and a chest of high-level gear once you reach level 60 again. This system keeps the open world populated and offers a reliable catch-up mechanic for alts.

For players focusing strictly on endgame progression, the primary daily activity revolves around "Call Boards" located in capital cities. These boards offer daily quests that reward Runes of Ascension and progress a reward bar. Completing this bar awards a box of high-tier raid loot, serving as an exceptional catch-up mechanic.

| Endgame Activity | Group Size | Primary Rewards | Key Mechanics |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Call Boards** | Solo / Group | Runes of Ascension, Raid Gear Boxes | Daily questing, profession turn-ins, and gold auto-completion. |
| **Heroic Dungeons** | 5-man | Pre-raid blue gear, emblem currencies | Requires a minimum item level to queue via RDF. |
| **Mythic Plus (M+)** | 5-man | Upgradable gear, custom currencies | Requires physical travel to the dungeon to activate the pedestal. |
| **Raiding (Zul'Gurub, Molten Core)** | 10 / 40-man | Tier sets, legendary items, tokens | Modernized boss mechanics tailored to custom CoA classes. |

Endgame PvE also features a robust Mythic+ system. Players can physically travel to dungeons, insert a key into the pedestal, and tackle scaling difficulties. Items dropped in Mythic+ can be upgraded multiple times using custom currencies, allowing dedicated players to optimize their gear slots perfectly. These systems were heavily tested prior to the final **conquest of azeroth open beta date** transition, ensuring a smooth raiding environment at launch.

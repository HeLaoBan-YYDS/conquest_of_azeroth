## The Evolution of Conquest of Azeroth

The development journey of Conquest of Azeroth (CoA) spans several years of rigorous testing, transitioning from early alpha builds to polished beta environments. Initially launched as a concept featuring completely custom hero classes, the project has undergone massive structural changes to improve gameplay fluidity. 

A major milestone occurred in late 2022 when the developers rolled out a massive Core Rework. Based on player experience at the time, the early game progression felt somewhat disjointed because classes had too many abilities competing for attention. The rework streamlined these kits, ensuring each specialization felt distinct, impactful, and satisfying from level one.

In 2023, the team introduced a comprehensive Talent Overhaul. The user interface was modernized to mimic the talent tree structure seen in retail expansions like Dragonflight. This visual clarity allowed players to theorycraft complex builds easily, choosing talents that synergize perfectly with their chosen roles. 

As the project transitioned into the **conquest of azeroth beta** cycles, the focus shifted from basic system mechanics to end-game balance, class identity, and server stability. Regular open beta weekends have allowed the developers to gather massive amounts of data from thousands of concurrent players.

## Custom Classes and Specializations

The primary draw of this custom server is the massive variety of custom classes. Traditional World of Warcraft limits players to a handful of archetypes, but CoA introduces 21 completely custom options. Each of these classes features three distinct specializations, totaling 69 unique ways to play.

Let us look at some of the standout classes available during the testing phases:
*   **Felsworn:** Chaos-wielding melee/caster hybrids that embrace demonic corruption.
*   **Guardian:** A stalwart protector using holy and physical barriers to shield allies.
*   **Witch Doctor:** Utilizing voodoo, mojo, hexes, and shadow magic to heal or decay enemies.
*   **Bloodmage:** A high-risk caster who uses their own health pool to cast devastating spells.
*   **Necromancer:** A master of plague, disease, and summoning undead minions.
*   **Cultist:** Utilizing void energy and dark whispers to manipulate the battlefield.

During the **conquest of azeroth beta** testing phases, players are encouraged to try as many of these classes as possible to help the developers balance the scaling of custom abilities. Community reports indicate that finding the right class combination is half the fun, with players sharing builds, rotation guides, and gear lists on community forums.

## Progression Phases and Level Caps

Project Ascension uses a phased release strategy to ensure that each section of the game receives adequate testing. Instead of launching a level 60 server immediately, developers hold targeted beta weekends with restricted level caps. This approach keeps the player base concentrated in specific zones, making it easier to identify bugs and scaling issues.

For example, early beta tests set a level cap of 35, allowing players to focus heavily on early-game RPG elements and mid-tier talent combinations. Later, other test phases pushed the cap to level 55 on a fresh-start event realm. These fresh starts are highly popular because they put all players on an equal footing.

By the time the **conquest of azeroth beta** reached its June open test, players were able to experience the full leveling curve up to level 60 and test high-end abilities. These structured phases prevent players from burning out on the leveling process while giving the developers clean, bracket-specific data.

Player experience reports suggest that these limited-time level brackets foster a highly competitive environment. Players optimize their gear for the level 35 or 55 brackets, creating unique mini-metas for PvP battlegrounds and dungeons.

## Endgame PvE: Raids and Dungeons

Reaching the level cap in the **conquest of azeroth beta** is only the beginning of the adventure. The server features fully customized versions of iconic vanilla dungeons and raids. Because players have access to 69 unique specializations, classic encounters must be re-tuned to remain challenging.

Iconic raids like Molten Core and Zul'Gurub have been adapted for the custom class ecosystem. In Molten Core, players must coordinate their custom abilities to defeat Ragnaros and his minions. The raid drops legendary items that are highly coveted by the player base.

Community reports emphasize that traditional raid compositions do not apply here. A raid group might feature a Necromancer tanking, a Bloodmage healing, and a Felsworn dealing burst DPS. This diversity requires raid leaders to re-evaluate their strategies and adapt to the unique strengths of each custom class.

The developers closely monitor these raid clears during the **conquest of azeroth beta** to adjust boss health pools, ability damage, and mechanical triggers. This ensures that when the server officially launches, the PvE progression feels rewarding and fair.

## How to Join and What to Expect

If you are ready to jump into the action, getting started is simple. The developers regularly host free open beta weekends, which are announced on their official news feed. During these events, anyone can download the custom client and start playing immediately without purchasing a bundle.

To participate, head over to the official [Project Ascension News Page](https://ascension.gg/en/news/conquest-of-azeroth) to register an account and download the launcher. The launcher will automatically download the necessary custom patches, models, and textures required to display the custom classes correctly.

When logging into the **conquest of azeroth beta**, you should expect a dynamic testing environment. Because the server is in beta, you may encounter occasional bugs, balance adjustments, and server restarts. Providing constructive feedback on the official forums or Discord is highly encouraged to help shape the game.

Additionally, keep an eye out for seasonal events like Hallow's End or Winterveil. Participating in these events during the beta often rewards players with exclusive cosmetic pets, mounts, or titles that carry over to future test phases or the live server.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is the Conquest of Azeroth beta free to play?
Yes, during scheduled open beta weekends and public stress tests, anyone can play for free. However, permanent access during closed testing phases may require purchasing an alpha/beta bundle from the official store.

### What makes the classes in this project unique?
Unlike standard World of Warcraft, which uses traditional classes like Mages and Warriors, this project features 21 completely custom classes built from Warcraft lore, such as the Witch Doctor, Felsworn, and Bloodmage.

### How do I earn rewards during the conquest of azeroth beta?
Players can earn exclusive cosmetic rewards, such as unique pets and titles, by participating in specific open beta events and reaching designated level milestones.

### Will my progress carry over to the live server?
Generally, progress made on beta and event realms is wiped before the official release to ensure a fair launch. However, any cosmetic rewards earned are linked to your account and will persist.

## Core Mechanics of the Custom UI and Ammunition

When you first load up a **conquest of azeroth tinker** character, you will immediately notice a custom user interface element. This peculiar custom UI tracks your standard ammunition, which is the lifeblood of your damage output. Unlike traditional hunters who rely on passive ammo slots, the Tinker actively generates and consumes ammunition as a core combat resource.

The Tinker uses Energy as its primary resource, but your most powerful shots are strictly gated by your ammunition count. Your maximum ammunition capacity is eight standard rounds. Managing this resource on the move is the key to maintaining high DPS.

Here is a breakdown of the foundational starter abilities:

*   **Rifle:** Equips your basic gun. Whenever you equip a new weapon, you automatically cast Reload one time.
*   **Black Powder Shot:** Fires your currently activated gun, consuming one standard ammunition. This is your primary spammable attack, dealing physical damage that scales directly with your Agility.
*   **Reload:** Generates four standard ammunition. This ability can be cast while moving, ensuring you never have to stand still to replenish your arsenal.

One of the most defining aspects of the Tinker is the complete absence of an auto-shoot mechanic. You control every single shot manually. This design choice prevents passive damage but rewards highly active players with unparalleled mobility. You can run in circles, kiting enemies indefinitely while spamming Black Powder Shot and reloading on the fly. 

To maximize your damage, Agility is your primary stat. It directly increases your physical damage, critical strike chance, and ranged attack power. Furthermore, Tinkers possess excellent defensive versatility, as they are proficient in wearing Cloth, Leather, and Mail armor.

## Mastering the Sentry Turrets and Scaling

According to community reports and early player experience, the **conquest of azeroth tinker** excels at defensive playstyles and area denial. This strength is entirely driven by the **Build Sentry Turret** ability unlocked at level 10. 

Because the turret has a 40-second cooldown and a 2-minute duration, a clever player can have up to three turrets active simultaneously. However, maintaining three active turrets requires precise timing, as your first turret will only have a 40-second remaining duration by the time you place the third.

| Turret Number | Time Placed | Cooldown Status | First Turret Remaining Duration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Turret 1** | 0 seconds | Cooldown starts (40s) | 120 seconds |
| **Turret 2** | 40 seconds | Cooldown starts (40s) | 80 seconds |
| **Turret 3** | 80 seconds | Cooldown starts (40s) | 40 seconds |
| **Turret 4** *(Replaces Turret 1)* | 120 seconds | Cooldown starts (40s) | 0 seconds (Turret 1 expires) |

In PvP environments like Warsong Gulch, stacking your turrets in a tight cluster is highly recommended. Player experiences suggest that stacking turrets improves the pet AI's targeting consistency, ensuring all three turrets focus down the same target simultaneously. 

While the exact scaling parameters of the sentry turrets are not explicitly detailed in the current alpha UI, community testing indicates they inherit a portion of the Tinker's ranged attack power and critical strike chance. The turrets also possess a respectable health pool (roughly equal to the player's maximum health), making them difficult for single-target damage dealers to quickly clear.

## Unleashing Explosive Combos in PvE and PvP

If you want to master the **conquest of azeroth tinker**, you must understand how to chain your utility spells and explosive devices. The Tinker is far more than a physical ranged DPS; it is a master of fire-based burst damage.

Your primary burst setup revolves around the interaction between **Sapper Charge** and **Gunsling**. Sapper Charge attaches a bomb to the enemy for 10 seconds. When you hit that target with Gunsling, the charge detonates, dealing fire damage. Crucially, the explosion damage scales higher the closer the Sapper Charge is to naturally expiring. To get the absolute maximum damage, you must wait until the 9th second of the debuff before firing your Gunsling.

Additionally, **Sticky Bomb** is completely off the Global Cooldown (GCD). This means you can throw a Sticky Bomb mid-cast or immediately after firing a Black Powder Shot without interrupting your rotation.

| Step | Action | Timing | Tactical Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Step 1** | Deploy Sentry Turrets | Pre-combat setup | Establishes a safe zone and passive DPS. |
| **Step 2** | Cast Sapper Charge | 0 seconds | Starts the 10-second detonation timer on the target. |
| **Step 3** | Spam Black Powder Shot & Reload | 1 to 8 seconds | Kites the target while wearing down their health pool. |
| **Step 4** | Throw Sticky Bomb | 6 seconds | Places a 3-second delayed bomb that explodes at the 9-second mark. |
| **Step 5** | Cast Gunsling | 9 seconds | Triggers the maximum-damage Sapper Charge explosion simultaneously with the Sticky Bomb blast. |

This combo results in a massive dual-explosion that can easily obliterate unsuspecting players in PvP or instantly clear packs of elite mobs in dungeons.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best primary stat for a conquest of azeroth tinker?
Agility is the absolute best primary stat for the **conquest of azeroth tinker**. It directly increases your physical ranged damage, critical strike chance, and ranged attack power. While your explosive devices deal fire damage, your overall rotation is heavily reliant on physical gunshots and rapid ammunition consumption, making Agility far superior to Intellect or Spell Power.

### How do you play a conquest of azeroth tinker in PvP?
In PvP, the **conquest of azeroth tinker** is best played as a highly mobile kiter and defensive zone-controller. Set up your Sentry Turrets in key choke points, such as flag rooms or narrow pathways, to force enemies to fight on your terms. Use your high mobility to run while shooting, and rely on the Sapper Charge and Sticky Bomb combo to burst down targets before they can close the distance.

### Can the Tinker heal or tank on Project Ascension?
Yes. While the Firearms and Invention trees focus entirely on ranged DPS and explosive area denial, the Mechanics tree introduces defensive utility and engineering marvels. Players can build healing bots or pilot heavily armored Mech Suits, allowing the Tinker to branch out into off-healing and off-tanking roles depending on how they allocate their talent points.

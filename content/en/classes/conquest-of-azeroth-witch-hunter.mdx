## Weapon Mechanics and Playstyle Adaptability

One of the defining features when playing the **conquest of azeroth witch hunter** is your choice of weapon. Unlike traditional classes that are locked into a single combat style, this class completely shifts its playstyle depending on what you equip in your hands. 

If you equip a light crossbow, you gain the unique ability to fire your abilities while moving. This allows you to kite dangerous enemies indefinitely, peppering them with quick bolts while staying completely out of harm's way. 

For players who prefer raw, explosive power from a distance, equipping a rifle changes the pace of combat. Rifles slow down your attack speed but reward you with massive, shadow-infused blasts that can decimate an enemy's health pool from long range. 

Finally, stepping into melee range with one-handed or two-handed weapons transforms you into a frontline zealot or a heavily armored tank. This weapon adaptability ensures that you can always adjust your combat style to match the challenges you face.

## Deep Dive into the Four Specializations

Choosing a specialization defines your role as a **conquest of azeroth witch hunter**. The class features four distinct talent trees, allowing you to build your character as a mobile physical damage dealer, a shadow-casting marksman, a holy melee zealot, or a shadow-infused tank.

### Bolt Slinger (Mobile Ranged DPS)

The Bolt Slinger trade heavy firearms for light crossbows, focusing on speed and continuous movement. They use setup abilities to lock down targets before unleashing a barrage of debilitating bolts. 

Because almost all of their abilities can be cast on the move, Bolt Slingers excel at maintaining high damage output during mechanics-heavy boss encounters. They build up momentum as they move, which directly increases their movement speed and overall damage output.

### Darkness (Heavy Shadow DPS)

Darkness specialists augment their firearms with dark magic. Instead of physical bolts, they load their rifles with shadow-infused ammunition and build up stacks of black powder to trigger explosive finishing moves. 

This spec also features a pet mechanic, allowing you to summon a faithful Shadow Hound companion. This companion works in tandem with your ranged attacks, locking down targets while you prepare your heaviest rifle blasts.

### Inquisitor (Melee DPS)

The Inquisitor is a melee zealot that dances between two powerful combat stances: Purity and Wickedness. In Purity stance, you focus on rapid melee strikes to build up stacks of a resource called "sensed evil." 

Once your stacks are fully charged, you swap to Wickedness stance to unleash the flames of sin. This consumes your built-up stacks to deal devastating fire damage and leave the target vulnerable to future Holy and Fire attacks.

### Black Knight (Shadow Tank)

The Black Knight is a shadow-infused tank that wields a massive two-handed weapon. Rather than relying solely on armor and shields, Black Knights protect themselves by parrying incoming attacks and stealing life directly from their foes. 

They apply a persistent damage-over-time effect called Shadow Brand to their targets. They can then consume this brand to restore a massive portion of their own health, giving them unparalleled self-sustain.

## Optimal Rotations and Ability Synergies

To maximize your damage output, the **conquest of azeroth witch hunter** relies on chaining specific abilities to trigger powerful synergies. Below, we break down the core rotations for both ranged and melee setups.

### Ranged DPS Rotations

For Bolt Slingers, the rotation begins with Coiling Shot. This ability acts as your primary setup tool, slowing the target and preparing them for your follow-up attacks. Once the target is set, you apply Tormentor Bolt to deal shadow damage over time. You then weave in Witching Bolt to leech health and maintain your own survivability. For groups of enemies, you transition into Ricochet and Dark Frenzy to clear out packs quickly.

Darkness players follow a slower, more deliberate priority list. You begin by ordering your Shadow Hound to attack, using Hound Master's Call to summon additional spectral beasts. While your pets distract the target, you build up black powder stacks with your basic shots before releasing a devastating Witch Blaster barrage to finish them off.

| Spec | Opener | Main Builder | Finisher / Spammer | AOE Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bolt Slinger** | Coiling Shot | Tormentor Bolt | Witching Bolt | Ricochet -> Dark Frenzy |
| **Darkness** | Hound Master's Call | Shadow Rifle Shots | Witch Blaster | Black Powder Explosions |

### Melee and Tanking Rotations

Inquisitors must master the timing of their stance swaps. You start in Purity stance, utilizing your enhanced melee haste to rapidly strike the target and generate sensed evil. The moment you hit maximum stacks, you swap to Wickedness stance. 

In Wickedness, you immediately consume those stacks to inflict fire damage, making the target highly vulnerable. While the vulnerability window is active, you strike with Searing Blade and Smite Evil to maximize your damage.

Black Knights focus entirely on maintaining their defensive cycle. Your priority is to keep Shadow Brand active on all nearby targets. When your health begins to dip, you cast Desecrate to consume the active Shadow Brands, instantly healing yourself. You supplement this healing with ancient tonics to survive high-damage phases.

| **Wickedness Stance** | Searing Blade | Smite Evil | Fire/Holy Vulnerability |
| **Tanking (Active)** | Shadow Brand | Desecrate (Heal) | Ancient Tonics / Parries |

## Gear, Stats, and Custom Progression Tips

Gearing your **conquest of azeroth witch hunter** requires a clear understanding of your chosen specialization's primary stats. Because this class bridges the gap between physical attacks, shadow magic, and holy fire, your stat priority will shift dramatically.

Bolt Slingers and Inquisitors heavily favor Agility and Melee Haste. Agility increases your physical attack power and critical strike chance, while Haste allows Inquisitors to build sensed evil stacks much faster. 

Darkness specialists, on the other hand, look for gear that boosts Shadow Spell Power alongside Agility, as a large portion of their rifle damage is converted into Shadow magic. 

Black Knights prioritize Strength, Stamina, and Parry rating to ensure they can survive heavy hits while tanking dungeons.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best leveling spec for a conquest of azeroth witch hunter?
Player experience indicates that the Bolt Slinger is the most efficient spec for leveling. The ability to shoot while moving allows you to kite mobs continuously without taking damage, drastically reducing your downtime between pulls.

### Can the Black Knight spec main-tank raid content?
Yes, community reports confirm that the Black Knight is a highly viable main tank. Their combination of high parry rates, self-healing through Desecrate, and damage mitigation from ancient tonics allows them to handle boss mechanics effectively.

### How does the stance-dancing mechanic work for Inquisitors?
Inquisitors must build stacks of sensed evil in Purity stance using fast melee attacks. Once stacked, they switch to Wickedness stance to consume the stacks, dealing heavy fire damage and debuffing the target to take increased Holy and Fire damage.

### Do Darkness witch hunters require pet management skills?
Yes, since the Darkness specialization relies heavily on the Shadow Hound companion and abilities like Hound Master's Call, keeping your pet alive and micro-managing its targets is essential to maximizing your DPS.

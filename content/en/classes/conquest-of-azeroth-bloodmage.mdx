## The Four Paths: Specialization Guide

The true beauty of this class lies in its four distinct specializations [1.1]. Whether you want to heal your allies by sacrificing your own flesh, melt foes from afar, shred targets in close-quarters combat, or transform into a giant beast to tank bosses, there is a specialization designed for you [1.1].

### 1. Fleshweaver (The Ritualistic Healer)

Fleshweavers are not your typical holy priests. They are ritualistic healers who weave flesh and blood to mend wounds through damage [1.1]. By dealing damage, you fuel powerful healing effects on your nearby allies [1.1].

*   **Primary Stats:** Spirit, Stamina [1.1]
*   **Armor:** Leather [1.1]
*   **Core Mechanics:**
    *   **Pooled Vitality:** As you heal and deal damage, you build up stacks of Pooled Vitality [1.1]. This increases your overall healing output and empowers your core spells [1.1].
    *   **10-Stack Burst:** Upon reaching 10 stacks of Pooled Vitality, you unlock devastatingly powerful Area of Effect (AoE) heals that drain the life essence of surrounding enemies [1.1].
    *   **Sustained Group Healing:** Excellent for dungeons and raids where damage is constant, as your healing output intensifies the longer and more chaotic the combat becomes [1.1].

### 2. Sanguine (The Sanguine Spellcaster)

If you prefer to incinerate your enemies with crimson fire and blood-soaked devastation, the **conquest of azeroth bloodmage** Sanguine specialization is your calling. This spec is a pure ranged caster that transmutes their own life force into terrifying spells [1.1].

*   **Primary Stats:** Intellect, Stamina, Spirit
*   **Armor:** Leather [1.1]
*   **Core Mechanics:**
    *   **Health as a Resource:** You spend your own health to cast high-impact spells, leaving you constantly managing your HP bar [1.1].
    *   **The Hunger State:** As you cast, you enter a "Hunger" state [1.1]. Your damage increases significantly, but your health drains rapidly [1.1]. If you let this get out of hand, you become insatiable, bleeding every second [1.1].
    *   **Sanity & Leeching:** To regain your sanity and stabilize your health, you must use spells like *Vampiric Fang* and *Life Drinker* to leech life back from your foes [1.1].

### 3. Accursed (The Vampiric Hybrid)

For players who love high-mobility, hybrid gameplay, playing a **conquest of azeroth bloodmage** Accursed build provides a unique cycle of range and melee. You begin the fight as a caster to build up your rage, and once you are fully enraged, you transform into a monstrous Vampir [1.1].

*   **Primary Stats:** Agility, Stamina [1.1]
*   **Armor:** Leather [1.1]
*   **Core Mechanics:**
    *   **Rage Generation:** Cast ranged spells from safety to build up your rage pool [1.1].
    *   **Vampiric Transformation:** Once your rage is full, you transform into a Vampir and leap into close-quarters melee combat [1.1].
    *   **Bleed & Lifesteal Focus:** In your Vampir form, you deal brutal bleed damage, slash enemies, and gain massive lifesteal while taking reduced damage [1.1].
    *   **Polymorph Protection:** Recent patch updates have granted players in Accursed Form immunity to polymorph effects, making this spec a formidable threat in PvP environments.

### 4. Eternal / Lycanthropy (The Savage Tank)

Tanks who want to lead their allies as a relentless predator will find their home in the Eternal specialization (often referred to in player guides as the Lycanthropy spec) [1.1]. By shedding your mortal form, you become a massive, savage Worgen [1.1].

*   **Primary Stats:** Stamina, Agility [1.1]
*   **Armor:** Leather [1.1]
*   **Core Mechanics:**
    *   **Worgen Form:** Grants massive passive damage reduction and increases your maximum health pool [1.1].
    *   **Insatiable Lifesteal:** Your attacks heal you for a portion of the damage dealt, allowing you to self-sustain through the most punishing boss encounters [1.1].
    *   **Pack Leader:** You can summon a pack of spectral werewolves from the shadows to fight alongside you, distracting enemies and boosting your threat generation [1.1].

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How does the conquest of azeroth bloodmage perform in PvP?
Community reports suggest that the **conquest of azeroth bloodmage** is incredibly strong in PvP, particularly due to its high mobility, instant-cast crowd control, and self-sustain. The ability to spam a fear that simultaneously heals you, combined with polymorph immunity in transformed states, makes the class a nightmare to lock down in battlegrounds and arenas.

### Can any race play the conquest of azeroth bloodmage?
No, the **conquest of azeroth bloodmage** is restricted to specific races [1.1]. On the Alliance side, you can play as a Human or a Night Elf [1.1]. On the Horde side, you can select either a Blood Elf or a Troll [1.1]. Each of these races offers unique passive benefits that synergize with different specializations.

### What is the difference between the Eternal and Lycanthropy specs?
They are the exact same specialization [1.1]. The tanking spec is officially named "Eternal" on the talent tree, but because its core mechanic revolves around transforming into a savage Worgen and summoning werewolves, the community and developers frequently refer to it as the "Lycanthropy" spec [1.1].

### Is Spirit a useful stat for Sanguine damage mages?
Yes! While Intellect is your primary stat for spell power, Spirit is highly valued by Sanguine players. Because Sanguine spells constantly consume your own health, having high Spirit increases your combat health regeneration, helping you offset the self-inflicted damage and stay in your high-damage "Hunger" state longer.
